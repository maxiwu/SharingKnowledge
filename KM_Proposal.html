Proposal

AI-native knowledge management for software teams

A low-friction KM model that keeps knowledge in Git as Markdown, uses AI to extract and maintain content, and makes every answer traceable back to a source record.

No database Markdown + Git only AI drafts, humans approve Pull-first, minimal push

What this solves

Teams lose time rediscovering decisions, hunting for the right person, or trusting stale answers. This model turns chats, PRs, docs, meetings, and selected code signals into reusable knowledge cards that stay auditable, fresh, and lightweight enough to adopt.

  • Ask AI first: Answers come from KM with source links and freshness indicators.
  • Record the source trail: Every card can be traced to people, places, and time.
  • Keep it small: One card equals one decision, one procedure, or one concept.

Design principles

  • AI-native: AI extracts, drafts, and proposes updates.
  • Git-first: Markdown files are the source of truth.
  • Low friction: No database, no custom backend in phase 1.
  • Traceable: Every card has source records and TTL.
  • Safe: Humans do not edit published cards directly.

Operating model

1. Pull first. People ask the AI in chat or IDE. The assistant answers from KM when it can.
2. Capture gaps. If the answer is missing, uncertain, out of scope, or conflicting, the AI creates or updates an open question.
3. Convert human answers into knowledge. A correct answer from chat or a meeting becomes a source record and triggers a KM update request.
4. Review where needed. AI drafts updates; designated reviewers approve sensitive or high-impact changes before publish.

Scope

  • In scope: software, architecture, inspection process, inspection data, inspection knowledge.
  • Out of scope: trade secrets, hardware specs, pricing, schedules.

The pilot should stay narrow so the team can learn fast without trying to ingest everything at once.

Knowledge record format

Every card should carry minimum metadata: title, domain tags, status, owner, reviewers, source records, last updated time, TTL, related questions, and related cards. Source records capture who said it, where it came from, when it was captured, and whether the content is AI-drafted or human-validated.

Governance

  • Human edits are indirect: People request changes through AI rather than editing KM files directly.
  • Conflicts are explicit: AI flags contradictions instead of merging them silently.
  • Freshness matters: TTL makes expired cards visible as stale and reviewable.
  • Weekly cleanup: A light review pass handles stale or conflicting items.

Cheat sheets and push model

AI can maintain role-based cheat sheets from validated cards only. The push model stays minimal: one weekly team view with open questions by age, stale cards, and top recurring questions.

Pilot success criteria

30recurring questions answered by KM
80%of answers marked useful by askers
95%of cards with source records and TTL
95%of open questions assigned within 1 business day
80%of stale cards reviewed before expiry
5dmax for critical questions to remain unresolved

Start with one team, seed from recent high-signal sources, and expand only after the pilot proves useful.
